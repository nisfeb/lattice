How it works

lattice is two pieces: a tiny Gall agent on your ship, and a native app you point at it. Drop a gemtext file in your ship's /pub and anyone on the network can read it — instantly, straight from your ship.

the desk
%lattice on your ship
Publishes /pub/*.gmi to the Urbit namespace and serves it to other ships over remote scry. Follows files you subscribe to.
the app
browse & edit anywhere
Android + desktop, one codebase. Reads urb:// pages, edits your own, and gets live notifications when a followed file changes.

What it does

  • Browse urb:// Fetch and read gemtext published by any ship, peer-to-peer over Urbit's remote scry. No server in the middle — the bytes come straight from the author's ship.
  • Publish Anything in /pub/*.gmi on your ship is instantly readable by anyone as urb://~you/that/path. Edit it from inside the app.
  • Built-in editor Write and edit your pages without leaving the app. Plain by default; flip on vim keybindings if that's your thing.
  • Browser tabs Multiple urb:// pages open at once, Ctrl+T for a new one, bookmarks for the pages you keep coming back to.
  • Follow & subscribe Follow ships to see what they publish; subscribe to a single file and get a live push the moment it changes — over an Eyre SSE channel, not polling.
  • Discovery Find other lattice publishers among the ships in your %contacts, via a small manifest each publisher exposes.
  • Copy to your ship Like a bookmark, but real: pull a remote file down onto your own ship at a path you choose.
  • Fully themeable Colors and fonts are yours to set. Ships with several built-in themes — Lattice Dark, Light, and more.
  • Native everywhere Android, Linux, macOS, Windows from one Kotlin / Compose Multiplatform codebase. Desktop builds bundle their own JRE.

What it isn't

  • Not a host. Bring your own ship — yours, a friend's, or a hosted one.
  • Not the HTTP web. Pages are Urbit-native (urb://~ship/path) and move between ships over remote scry, not over DNS and HTTP.
  • Not on the app stores. Sideload the APK and desktop installers from GitHub Releases.
  • Desktop builds are unsigned for now — Gatekeeper / SmartScreen will warn on first launch and you have to bypass it.

Privacy

The only server lattice talks to is your ship.

No analytics, no third-party SDKs, no telemetry. The app speaks to the Urbit ship you point it at — that's the entire network surface. Your session never leaves the device unencrypted: lattice refuses to send your +code or cookie in cleartext to a remote ship, and stores it owner-only at rest.

Everything you put in /pub is public by design — that's the whole point of a publishing tool. Nothing else on your ship is exposed.

Under the hood

The %lattice Gall agent publishes via Clay → %grow and answers remote scry; the app speaks an authenticated HTTP API to your ship over OkHttp, with live updates on an Eyre SSE channel. Kotlin + Compose Multiplatform for the UI, one shared codebase across Android and JVM desktop. Hoon unit tests on the agent, a JVM test suite on the app, both gated in CI.
